# District 3 — Mandate & Organisational Context

## What D3 is

District 3 (D3) is a startup incubator and innovation hub embedded within Concordia University in downtown Montréal, at 1250 Guy Street. Founded in 2014, it has supported over 1,400 startups and grown into one of Quebec's leading incubators.

D3 is **non-profit, publicly funded, and open to all Quebec residents** — not just Concordia students or alumni. Services are entirely free. No equity is taken. Founders retain full intellectual property.

## Core mandate

D3's mandate is to support **Quebec-based entrepreneurship** — helping innovators move from initial spark to real-world impact. It nurtures multidisciplinary teams and scientific entrepreneurs who harness emerging technologies to create businesses with global impact. The emphasis is on purpose-driven innovation: ideas that are not just commercially viable but meaningfully address real problems.

## Who D3 serves

- Entrepreneurs at **all stages**: idea through to scale
- **All sectors and industries** are welcome, though D3 has specialised expertise in four streams (see streams document)
- Quebec residents — programs require in-person participation in Quebec
- Concordia students and researchers receive some dedicated programming, but the community is broader
- English working proficiency is required for cohort programs

## What D3 is not assessing at intake

Per D3's own FAQ: *"At this stage, we're not assessing the viability or quality of your startup — our focus is on ensuring we can add value to your journey."*

The intake review is not a judgment of whether the startup will succeed. It is an assessment of **fit**: will D3's programs benefit this team, at this stage, in this sector?

## Organisational values

- **Coachability** — openness to feedback is an explicit evaluation criterion
- **Collaborative innovation** — the community model is central; D3 is an ecosystem, not just a program
- **Equity and inclusion** — publicly funded, with active DEI reporting. Demographic data is collected for accountability purposes only and must never influence admissions decisions
- **Social and environmental responsibility** — ESG criteria and systems thinking are embedded across programs, not just in the Social stream
- **Indigenous land acknowledgment** — D3 operates on unceded Kanien'kehá:ka territory; this context informs its community values

## Key facts for application review

- ~200–250 applications per month
- ~250 startups accepted annually
- 2-week response promise to all applicants
- All programs are free; no equity taken; IP stays with founders
- NDA-bound — staff and consultants cannot disclose application content
