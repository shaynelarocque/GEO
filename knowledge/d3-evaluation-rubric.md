# District 3 — Application Evaluation Rubric

## Purpose of this document

This rubric defines the criteria used to evaluate incoming applications during the initial review (sniff test) stage. It is intended to help the agent produce a consistent, structured scorecard — not to make decisions. All decisions remain with ops and the Applications Panel.

Per D3's own FAQ, the intake review is not assessing whether a startup will succeed. It is assessing **fit**: can D3 add value to this team at this stage?

---

## Scoring system

Each criterion is scored as one of:

- **Met** — clear, verifiable evidence in the application or from external research
- **Partial** — some evidence but incomplete, ambiguous, or reliant on unverified claims
- **Unclear** — the application or research does not provide enough information to assess
- **Missing** — field is blank, criterion is clearly not met, or disqualifying condition present

---

## Criteria

### 1. Quebec / Canada eligibility
**What to check:** Is the startup building or expanding in Quebec? Is at least one founder legally permitted to work or study in Canada (citizen, PR, valid work permit, or student visa)?

- Met: Explicit "yes" to Canada registration, at least one founder with eligible status
- Partial: Planning to incorporate but not yet, or unclear province
- Missing: Founder selects "None of the above" for Canadian status — **this is a hard disqualifier**; D3 legally cannot serve this applicant

---

### 2. Clear problem statement
**What to check:** Does the applicant articulate a specific, real problem, need, or social issue? Is the target population or context identifiable?

- Met: Problem is concrete, specific, with a named population or context
- Partial: Problem is vague ("we want to improve healthcare") or too broad
- Unclear: Application describes a solution without a problem
- Missing: Field is blank or non-responsive

---

### 3. Solution exists or is being developed
**What to check:** Is there a proposed solution? D3 explicitly accepts pre-solution applicants, but there should be some directional thinking.

Note: D3's FAQ states it's okay if the applicant hasn't worked on a solution yet — they are still eligible for early-stage programs.

- Met: Solution is described, even if early-stage or conceptual
- Partial: Solution is very vague or appears to be a copy-paste from the problem field
- Missing: No solution described and no acknowledgment of early stage

---

### 4. Stream / sector alignment
**What to check:** Does the startup have a clear connection to one of D3's four streams (Bio, Healthcare, High Tech, Social Innovation)? All sectors are welcome, but stream placement determines program fit.

- Met: Clear fit with one stream; can be confidently assigned
- Partial: Borderline between two streams, or weak fit with any
- Unclear: Cannot determine stream from application alone
- Note: Flag low-confidence assignments with specific reasoning for ops

---

### 5. Team composition
**What to check:** Are there at least two co-founders? (Launch & Grow explicitly requires this; it is a strong signal for all programs.) Do the founders have relevant or complementary backgrounds?

- Met: Two or more founders, backgrounds are complementary or relevant
- Partial: Solo founder (eligible but weaker fit for cohort programs), or team composition is unclear
- Unclear: Only one person appears to have submitted, but team section suggests more exist
- Missing: Genuinely solo, no co-founder mentioned anywhere

---

### 6. Time commitment
**What to check:** Is at least one founder committing significant hours? What does the hours-per-week field show across founders?

- Met: At least one founder at 20+ hours/week; ideally 30+
- Partial: Both founders at 10–20 hours (workable but weak signal for intensive programs)
- Unclear: Inconsistent with stated stage (e.g., claims MVP but all founders at < 10 hrs)
- Missing: All founders at < 10 hours/week — significant flag for program readiness

Time commitment is a direct input to program placement:
- < 10 hrs: Likely pre-idea or casual exploration; may not benefit from intensive cohort
- 10–20 hrs: Suitable for Systems Mapping; borderline for Validation
- 20–30 hrs: Suitable for Validation
- 30+ hrs: Ready for Validation or Launch & Grow

---

### 7. Coachability signals
**What to check:** Does the application show any evidence of openness to feedback, iteration, or self-awareness about gaps? This is subjective and should be scored conservatively.

- Met: Applicant acknowledges uncertainty, mentions prior learning, or frames their needs in terms of growth
- Partial: Neutral — no strong signals either way
- Unclear: Application is very short or formulaic; can't assess

Do not score negatively for confidence. Only flag if the tone is explicitly closed ("we have figured everything out") in a way that seems brittle.

---

### 8. Differentiation / novelty
**What to check:** Is there something novel, differentiated, or innovative about the approach? D3 looks for "novel concept with clear potential for real-world application" and "innovative use of technology or research where appropriate."

- Met: Clear differentiation from obvious alternatives; some evidence of unique approach
- Partial: Novelty is claimed but not substantiated; "we're different because we care more"
- Unclear: Agent could not find comparable alternatives, so cannot assess differentiation
- Missing: Application appears to describe a well-established commodity with no novel angle

---

### 9. Prior incubator experience
**What to check:** Has the team participated in other incubators or entrepreneurship programs?

This is **not** a negative signal. Prior experience often means a more prepared team. Note it as context, not a filter.

- Flag if: Prior program appears to overlap significantly with what D3 offers (duplication risk)
- Flag if: Prior program was recent and the startup appears to be "program hopping"

---

## Hard disqualifiers (flag immediately, do not proceed with full brief)

1. **No eligible Canadian status** for any founder — D3 legally cannot serve this team
2. **Not building or planning to build in Quebec** — out of mandate
3. **English proficiency concern** — if application language suggests working proficiency may not be met (use judgment carefully; do not penalise non-native writers)

---

## Program fit signals (for classification suggestion)

| Signal | Suggested program |
|---|---|
| No solution yet; idea stage | Systems Mapping |
| Solution defined, no market validation | Validation |
| MVP or proof of concept; pilot evidence | Validation |
| Demonstrated traction; early revenue or users | Launch & Grow |
| Established company scaling or seeking CEO-level growth | MentorConnect |

High Tech note: proof of concept is NOT required for Validation in the High Tech stream specifically.

---

## What this rubric is not

- It is not a prediction of startup success
- It is not a DEI filter — demographic fields must never influence scoring
- It is not a replacement for the Applications Panel's judgment
- A low score does not mean automatic rejection — it means the panel needs more information
