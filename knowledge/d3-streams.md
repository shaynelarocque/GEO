# District 3 — Stream Definitions

D3 organises its sector-specific expertise into four streams. Every accepted startup is placed into one stream, which determines their coach, panel, peer cohort, and network access. Stream assignment is made by the Applications Panel — not by the applicant.

---

## Bio (Biotech)

**D3's positioning:** The only incubator in Quebec with specialised expertise in the bio sector.

**What it covers:**
- Biopharma
- Agri-food innovation
- Clean technologies with biological components
- Biomaterials

**Who fits:**
Founders using biological innovation to drive commercialisation and impact — on people or the planet. Research-to-market journeys are common here. Scientific or technical co-founders typical. IP strategy and regulatory pathway are frequent coaching themes.

**Example alumni signals:** Life sciences researchers, biotech spin-offs, sustainable materials companies, microbiome or fermentation-based startups (e.g., Hyasynth Bio — cannabinoids via yeast fermentation).

**Does NOT fit:**
- Pure software or digital products with no biological component
- Medical devices or digital health tools (→ Healthcare stream)
- Social impact organisations without a bio-tech core

---

## Healthcare

**D3's positioning:** Empowering startups in medical devices, digital health, and paramedical solutions at every stage.

**What it covers:**
- Medical devices
- Digital health (apps, platforms, data tools serving clinical or patient populations)
- Paramedical solutions
- Health education technology
- AI applied to healthcare workflows

**Who fits:**
Founders whose primary customer or beneficiary is within the healthcare system — patients, clinicians, hospitals, health insurers, or public health bodies. Regulatory awareness (Health Canada, FDA pathways) is often relevant. Clinical validation matters.

**Example alumni signals:** Medtech hardware, telemedicine platforms, clinical decision support tools, VR training for healthcare professionals (e.g., UbiSim — VR nursing simulation).

**Does NOT fit:**
- Wellness or fitness apps without clinical application (likely → High Tech or Social)
- Biotech or drug development (→ Bio stream)
- Social determinants of health without a health-system delivery component (→ Social Innovation)

**Edge case:** A startup addressing food insecurity through a nutritional health lens may fit either Healthcare or Social — stream lead judgment required.

---

## High Tech

**D3's positioning:** Helping ambitious technology founders accelerate toward product-market fit through tailored, one-on-one coaching.

**What it covers:**
- Software and SaaS products
- AI/ML applications (outside of pure healthcare or bio applications)
- Hardware and robotics
- Fintech, edtech, climate tech with strong tech core
- Any startup where technology is the core innovation

**Who fits:**
Founders where the primary differentiation is a novel use of technology. Market validation and go-to-market are key coaching themes. High Tech is the only stream where **proof of concept is not required** for the Validation program — early-stage tech ideas are explicitly welcome.

**Example alumni signals:** AI platforms, SaaS tools, robotics, drone tech, data analytics products (e.g., Whale Seeker — AI for whale detection from aerial imagery; CANN Forecast — AI for municipal water quality).

**Does NOT fit:**
- Social enterprises whose tech is incidental to the mission (→ Social Innovation)
- Digital health with a clinical user base (→ Healthcare)
- Biotech with software components (→ Bio, unless software is the primary product)

---

## Social Innovation

**D3's positioning:** Supporting startups focused on social issues who want positive impact on community and the world, guided by ESG criteria.

**What it covers:**
- Education
- Environment and sustainability
- Culture
- Community health and social services
- Any startup where the primary purpose is social or environmental impact

**Who fits:**
Founders where mission and impact are the central organising principle — not just a secondary consideration. Social enterprises, non-profits building revenue models, and impact-first companies. ESG and systems thinking coaching are embedded. The Social stream at D3 is not a lower-tier option — it is a serious stream with dedicated expertise.

**Example alumni signals:** Stop Cyberviolences (online harm reduction), DocTocToc (social paediatrics for vulnerable families), Emission Reduction Now (climate), Cedulify (occupational therapy / mental health access).

**Does NOT fit:**
- Startups that mention social impact but are fundamentally commercial tech products (→ High Tech)
- Healthcare delivery organisations without a community/social-determinants angle (→ Healthcare)

## Borderline / Multi-stream cases

Some startups span streams. Common patterns:

| Pattern | Likely stream | Note |
|---|---|---|
| Health tech for underserved communities | Social Innovation or Healthcare | Depends on primary customer: system or community |
| Environmental biotech | Bio | If biology is the core innovation |
| Climate SaaS | High Tech | If software is the differentiation |
| Mental health app | Healthcare or High Tech | Depends on clinical vs consumer framing |
| Agri-food with social mission | Bio or Social | Ask: is the innovation biological or organisational? |

When stream assignment confidence is below ~70%, flag for ops attention with specific reasoning.
