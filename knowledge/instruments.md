# Drafted accountability instruments

When a lens reaches `insufficient_evidence` at low confidence, the gap is the finding. You draft the appropriate instrument (ATIP request, Order Paper question, or committee follow-up) and surface it to the human reviewer for editing and submission.

**You never send. You never email. You never publish.** The tool drafts; a human decides. This is the architectural commitment of the system. There is no tool to dispatch any of these instruments.

## Choosing the instrument

| Gap looks like… | Instrument | Why |
|---|---|---|
| The dollar amount, recipient, or vendor for a specific transaction is not in any public source | **ATIP request** to the funding department | ATIP is the formal route for records held by a federal institution. Slow (~30 days statutory) but produces actual records. |
| A specific yes/no or a number that the minister or department should know | **Order Paper question** | OPQs are answered on the public record by the department. Faster than ATIP and produces a Hansard-citable answer. Requires an MP sponsor in real life; the draft is for a constituent to send to their MP. |
| The program is appearing before a committee soon, or a related committee study is open | **Committee follow-up** | Surface the gap as a question for committee members to put to officials in real time. Produces public testimony. Highest profile but only viable with active committee proceedings. |

## Common rules

- Address by **role**, never by name. "To: Health Canada ATIP Coordinator", not a specific person. Names change; roles persist; targeting people creates legal and dignity exposure the tool does not need.
- Include the **goal anchor** the request is anchored against. The recipient of the instrument should see why the question matters for accountability.
- Cite the **public sources you already checked**, so the recipient knows the request is not noise. You did the work and hit a wall.
- Keep requests narrow. One ATIP request asks for one well-defined record set. Bundling kills response time.

## ATIP request: template

> To: [Department] ATIP Coordinator
> From: [reviewer name, left blank for the human to fill]
> Date: [today]
>
> Re: Access to Information Act request, records pertaining to [program name]
>
> Pursuant to the Access to Information Act, I request the following records:
>
> 1. [Specific record class, e.g. "All contribution agreement amendments between Department X and Recipient Y for agreement number Z, including the amendment justification memos that accompany each amendment, for the period [date range]."]
> 2. [Continue per record class. Keep each item narrow.]
>
> **Context for triage:** This request is anchored against the program's stated commitment of [original_budget] over [timeline] to [stated_objectives, summarised]. Public sources I have already reviewed: [list with URLs]. The records above would resolve the gap between the public record and the stated commitment.
>
> Format preference: electronic, in original-record format where practicable.
>
> Fee: I understand the application fee of $5 applies. Records up to 5 hours of search and preparation are included.

## Order Paper question: template

> Suggested OPQ for sponsoring MP to table:
>
> "With regard to [program name], administered by [department]:
> (a) what is the [specific quantitative ask, e.g. 'total disbursement to Recipient Y for fiscal years 2018-19 through 2024-25, broken down by sub-program'];
> (b) [follow-up];
> (c) [follow-up];
> What was the original commitment, and what justification accompanied each amendment that increased the contribution above the original ceiling?"
>
> **Anchor for the MP's office:** This question is anchored against [stated_objectives, original_budget]. Public sources reviewed: [list]. Records the question is designed to put on the public record: [list].

## Committee follow-up: template

> Suggested question for [Standing Committee on …] members to put to [official's role, never name]:
>
> "[Plain-spoken question, short and pointed. The committee question is the headline; the public version of an ATIP.]"
>
> **Anchor and sources:** [as above]

## After drafting

Surface the draft to the human reviewer with the gap it addresses, the lens it came from, and the goal anchor it's measured against. The human decides whether to edit, submit, escalate, or shelve. The tool's job ends at the draft.
